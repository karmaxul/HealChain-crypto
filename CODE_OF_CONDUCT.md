# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

HealChain is built on the belief that basic data security is a human right. We extend that same principle to our community — everyone who contributes to, uses, or engages with HealChain deserves to be treated with respect and dignity.

We are committed to making participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, background, experience level, nationality, or identity.

---

## Our Standards

**We expect:**

- Respectful, constructive communication
- Welcoming feedback and differing viewpoints
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the mission
- Treating others the way you would want to be treated

**We do not tolerate:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- Discriminatory language or behavior
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Sustained disruption of discussions or pull request reviews
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all HealChain spaces — GitHub repositories, issue trackers, pull requests, email communications, and any other forum where community members interact in their capacity as contributors or users.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to [hello@healchain.org](mailto:hello@healchain.org). All reports will be reviewed confidentially and responded to appropriately.

Project maintainers who do not uphold this Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent removal from the project.
